Your Playbook
What a perfect score looks like in each section — and the exact words to get there.
01 Transfer Takeover — CLICK TO EXPAND
The gap: Sometimes misses referencing the customer's reason for shopping in the first 30 seconds.
A 5: Seamless takeover — greet by name, confirm key details, respond to their reason for shopping, and ask if they're ready to switch today. Zero friction from the first word.
"Hey [Name], I see you came in looking at [vehicle]. Sounds like you're shopping because [reason]. If I can get you a better rate today, are you looking to make the switch?"
PROOF: Dennis Cruz — smooth takeover, confirmed details without making him repeat anything.
02 Needs Discovery 2.1 / 5 CLICK TO EXPAND
The gap: You jump to quoting after 1-2 questions. No customer profile = no savings story = no close.
A 5: Full consultative household review. Ask about auto details, rent/own, other drivers, current carrier, budget. Uncover stated AND unstated needs — identify renters and offer it without being asked. Plant life seeds. Use the customer's own words later in the call.
"Before I put anything together, let me ask a few quick questions. What are you currently paying? Is it just you on the policy? Do you rent or own? Any tickets or accidents I should know about?"
PROOF: Dennis Cruz — you asked what he paid ($278), found his renters, built the full picture. That scored 21/25.
03 Value Presentation 2.3 / 5 CLICK TO EXPAND
The gap: One price, no anchor, no options. Customer says no → dead end.
A 5: Use the customer's own words and situation. Present total financial picture: single-line cost vs. multi-policy bundle savings. Good/better/best options. Address why they started shopping and frame the switching decision.
"You're paying $278 with State Farm. I have you at $187 — that's $91 a month, over $1,000 a year. Bundle renters for $15 and I drop auto another $40. You're saving $131/month and fully protected."
PROOF: Harold Carter — multi-car discovery, renters pivot, $303 down to $243. He bought because you stacked the value.
04 — FOCUS Objection Handling 2.4 / 5 best: 4 (Yolanda Bogan)
The gap: When customers push back, you accept it. The call ends one question too early. You don't preemptively frame value to prevent objections.
A 5: Preemptively frame value so the customer accepts it without objection. When they still push back, respond with a multi-layered rebuttal using details they shared earlier, reframe around their situation, and transition directly into a close attempt.
"Let me think about it" "Totally understand. Is it the price, or something else I can adjust? This rate is locked in today — I can't guarantee it tomorrow."
"No thank you" "Before you go — what are you paying now? I want to make sure you're not leaving money on the table."
"I'm shopping around" "I get it. This rate is competitive right now. If rates go up next week, this price is gone. Let me lock it in for you today."
"Talk to my spouse" "That's smart. I'll put together a summary showing the savings and hold this rate for 24 hours so you don't lose it."
PROOF: Rosher — "I know your insurance expires in two days, let's get this set up." That closed $2,160. You used urgency and their own situation to overcome hesitation.
05 — FOCUS Closing 3.0 / 5
The gap: 80% of calls end before you try to close. You don't transition to the close unless the customer does it for you. No trial closes throughout the call.
A 5: Closing feels like the natural end of a consultation. Use trial closes throughout the call, not just at the end. Present bind-now and bind-later options. Close on auto AND at least one additional line. Ask to save your number, opt into texts, and send a confirmation text.
"You're saving [amount] a month with better coverage. I can have your ID cards in your inbox in 10 minutes. Would you like to use a card or bank account?"
Trial close examples to use throughout: "If I can beat what you're paying, would you be ready to switch today?" — "Does this coverage sound like what you need?" — "So far so good?"
PROOF: Harold Carter — the full close + post-sale walkthrough. Used assumptive close, recapped savings, and walked through next steps. That's the template for every call.
